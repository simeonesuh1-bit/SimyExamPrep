### Sample Tricky Questions (8 Courses)

#### 1. PMG313 (Project Scope Management)
**Question**: During a Project Review, the technical lead suggests adding a small, low-risk encryption feature to the software that was not in the original Project Scope Statement. The customer was not aware of this, but it adds value. What is the most accurate term for this, and what is the PM's immediate duty?
- A. Scope Creep; immediately update the Change Log.
- B. Gold Plating; evaluate the impact on resources and report to the CCB.
- C. Value Engineering; incorporate it into the next iteration.
- D. Progressive Elaboration; document it as a 'Validated Deliverable'.
- **Correct**: B (Gold plating is adding value that wasn't requested; even if 'good', it risks budget/schedule).

#### 2. FIN313 (Financial Management)
**Question**: A firm is evaluating two mutually exclusive projects. Project X has an NPV of $50k and an IRR of 18%. Project Y has an NPV of $55k and an IRR of 15%. If the cost of capital is 10%, which project should be chosen for maximum shareholder wealth?
- A. Project X, because it has a higher Internal Rate of Return (IRR).
- B. Project Y, because it adds more absolute value (NPV) to the firm.
- C. Neither, as the Profitability Index must be calculated first for mutually exclusive sets.
- D. Both, if the company has sufficient capital.
- **Correct**: B (NPV is the gold standard for wealth maximization in mutually exclusive scenarios).

#### 3. BUA399 (Research Methods)
**Question**: You are conducting a study where the population is divided into subgroups (e.g., Seniors, Juniors, Sophomores) and you then pick a proportionate number of individuals from each group *non-randomly* to fill a specific count. This is:
- A. Stratified Random Sampling.
- B. Cluster Sampling.
- C. Quota Sampling.
- D. Systematic Sampling.
- **Correct**: C (Quota is the non-random version of stratified sampling).

#### 4. EHR305 (Compensation Management)
**Question**: Which compensation strategy is most likely to lead to high attraction and retention of 'A-Players' but increases the risk of 'Pay Compression' during periods of high inflation?
- A. Lag Strategy.
- B. Lead Strategy.
- C. Match Strategy.
- D. Individual Piece-Rate Strategy.
- **Correct**: B (Lead strategy attracts top talent but often results in new hires earning near or above existing veteran employees as market rates rise).

#### 5. BUA319 (E-Commerce)
**Question**: An e-commerce platform uses 'Dynamic Pricing' algorithms. If a customer is shown a higher price because they are visiting from an expensive city, this is a form of:
- A. First-Degree Price Discrimination.
- B. Second-Degree Price Discrimination.
- C. Third-Degree Price Discrimination.
- D. Market-Based Value Adjustment.
- **Correct**: C (Third-degree discrimination is based on group characteristics like location/segment).

#### 6. BUA313 (Innovation Management)
**Question**: A large firm fails to adopt a new, cheaper technology that satisfies its low-end customers but is ignored by its high-end customers. This phenomenon, which eventually leads to the firm's downfall as the technology improves, is known as:
- A. The Innovator's Dilemma.
- B. Product Cannibalization.
- C. The Chasm of Adoption.
- D. Technological Lock-in.
- **Correct**: A (Christensen's theory of disruptive innovation).

#### 7. BUA303 (Management Theory)
**Question**: According to Herzberg's Motivation-Hygiene Theory, which of the following actions will lead to a long-term increase in employee motivation?
- A. Increasing the annual salary by 15%.
- B. Improving the physical safety and lighting of the office.
- C. Providing more opportunities for personal growth and responsibility.
- D. Reducing the number of working hours.
- **Correct**: C (Achievement/Growth are 'Motivators'; Salary/Safety are 'Hygiene' factors that only prevent dissatisfaction).

#### 8. BUA317 (Entrepreneurship)
**Question**: An entrepreneur decides to change their business model from 'Subscription-Based' to 'Ad-Supported' after analyzing customer churn data. What is the technical term for this strategic shift?
- A. Market Penetration.
- B. Pivot.
- C. Diversification.
- D. Vertical Integration.
- **Correct**: B.

#### 9. PMG313 (Project Scope Management)
**Question**: What is the primary difference between 'Validate Scope' and 'Control Quality'?
- A. Validate Scope focuses on technical accuracy; Control Quality focuses on customer acceptance.
- B. Validate Scope is done at the end; Control Quality is done throughout.
- C. Validate Scope focuses on acceptance of deliverables; Control Quality focuses on correctness and meeting requirements.
- D. Validate Scope is internal; Control Quality is external.
- **Correct**: C.

#### 10. FIN313 (Financial Management)
**Question**: 'Capital Rationing' occurs when:
- A. A company has too many profitable projects but not enough funds to invest in all.
- B. A company is paying out all profits as dividends.
- C. The IRR of a project is less than the cost of capital.
- D. The stock market is in a bear phase.
- **Correct**: A.
